# What is Student Finance?
- System is changing for all years after ours
- Most common loans taken are tuition fee loans and maintenance
	- if you qualify for either, you qualify for both
	- Need to be UK citizen?
	- You may get extra support on top of this, either through bursaries, scholarships or directly through student finance

## Tuition fee loan
- Unis charge up to £9790/year
- Money goes towards teaching costs, facilities & support services
- Student never sees this money, gets paid directly to uni by Student Finance England
- Must be re-applied to for each year you are taking a course

## Maintenance loan
- Covers literally everything else, like:
	- Accom
	- Travel
	- Food
	- Books & equipment
	- etc
- Paid to students in 3 installments
	- However, the raw amount loaned to a student is impacted by:
		- Family Income
		- Where the student will be living (away from home or living at home)
		- Students living away from home will be entitled to a higher loan amount
		- There is a minimum amount that all students are entitled to, regardless of income
- If a parent/carer does not give their income info, student will only be entitled to the minimum amount of ~£5000
- www.gov.uk/student-finance-calculator - helps estimate entitlement amount
	- Rates apply to all students under 60
	- London students get a higher rate
- Bursaries and scholarships are always considered and applied to separately to a student finance loan
- Maximum of 4 years for these loans. For years past these, the funds are part bursary, part loan and part grant
- Part time job at the uni you go to? 
	- Fully flexible contract?
	- Maybe look at location transfer for Notcutts?
- **To find these scholarships/bursaries, just do a direct google search
- NB: The grants, bursaries and grants offered to students is uni dependant.
- Common Criteria for Bursaries & Scholarships:
	- Income
	- Academic achievement
	- Sports
	- Instruments
	- Ethnicity
	- Course
	- Personal circumstance (young carers)
	- Personal characteristics (LGBTQ+, etc)
## Applying
- Student makes account at www.gov.uk/apply-for-student-finance
- Apply online & submit any needed evidence
- Apply by May 15 2026 for September entry 
	- You can apply after this date, but you might not get your money before the start of term
	- Put uni & course you think you want to go to, can be changed later 
- Takes ~4 weeks to process with HMRC
### Maintenance Loan and 'Means-Tested'
- For this test, you need:
	- NI Number
	- Gets used by SFE (Student Finance England)
	- Income information - Based on previous tax year with HMRC (2024-25)
	- Also:
		- Paid into private pensions
		- Made additional voluntary contributions
		- Children (other than the student) who are financially dependant
- If income has fallen by %15< since last year, apply for a current year income assessment
- Income of whatever parent child is dependant on, regardless of marital status
- Includes parent partners
- SFE do not need income details from the parent that the child is not dependant on
- Next steps:
	- 6-8 weeks, SFE email will email CHILD 
	- Child consent needed for SFE for parent access
- Find out what 'Means-Tested' means
- Social work Bursary:
	- Selective 
	- Years 2 & 3 only
	- £4862.50 outside London
	- If not awarded, students get £862.50 travel allowance
- Disabled Student's Allowances (DSA):
	- Support for additional study costs due to disability, specific learning difficulty, mental health condition, or long-term illness
	- Might Cover:
		- Special equipment
		- Non-medical helpers
		- Other support
		- Extra travel to attend the course or placement due to disability
	- Required evidence:
		- Disability - Report/letter from doctor or consultant
		- Mental health condition - Report/letter from doctor or consultant. Diagnostic assessment from a practitioner psychology or specialist teacher

## Repaying your student loans
- Repayments are calculated based on what is affordable, not what is owed 
- The loan is not paid back until a student is earning above the repayment threshold
- The minimum threshold to repay a loan is currently £25,000
	- NB: This value is likely to change before graduation
- Repayments are 9% of your salary OVER this threshold
- Repayments are taken directly from wages if working in the UK
- Repayments start based on tax year start dates, not calendar
- You have 40 years to pay this amount back from the year you start repaying - After this 40 year period any remaining amount is written off.
- If earnings drop below the threshold, your payments will be paused, but the 40 year period does not stop
- Interest is applied to this loan based on the Retail Price Index (RPI)
