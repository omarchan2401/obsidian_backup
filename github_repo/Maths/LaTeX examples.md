LaTeX
### Inline Math:
This is inline math: $x^2 + y^2 = z^2$.

To do inline math, surround your equations with a dollar sign'$'

$\sqrt{x}$		


### Block Math(aka multiline math):
And this is block math:

To do block math, put 2 dollar signs on either side of your equation
$$
x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}
$$

$$
a^2 = b^2 +c^2 -2bc\cos(a)
$$
### Other Syntax:
- Fractions: each member of the set of integers can be expressed in the form $x = \frac{a}{b}$
- Superscripts: classic curve $y = x^2$ has one intersection with the x axis
- Subscripts: the arithmetic term $u_n = a + (n-1)d$
- Greek letters(just backslash and type them, capitalise first letter for capitals): $$\alpha * \beta = \theta - \lambda ^ \gamma -\Gamma$$

### Harder syntax:
- roots: $y = \sqrt{x}$
- summations: $\sum_{i=1}^{2n}(r^2 + 6r) = \frac{n}{2} -12$
- Integrals: $\int_0^\infty  (e^{-x}) dx$     or       $\int e{-2x} dx$
- Matrices: $\begin{bmatrix} a & b & 6 \\ c & d & e \\ 3 & 5 & \lambda \end{bmatrix}$
- Alignment: (double backslashes indicate a new line '&=' makes the equals signs line up)
$$
\begin{aligned}
3x^2 - 6x -2 &= 6x -5\\
3x^2 - 12x + 3 &= 0\\
x^2 - 4x +1 &= 0
\end{aligned}
 $$
